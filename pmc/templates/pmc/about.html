About

Mission

Unlock your KeyForge organized play with KeyChain

KeyChain is an unofficial, community-made platform built to supplement and enhance KeyForge organized play through digital rewards, record keeping, local and global leaderboards, personalization, and a progression system designed to encourage players to come back for more.

Progression & Ranking Systems

Global:
Elements that are account-wide, across all playgroups.
Playgroup:
Elements that are within the context of an individual playgroup.

Level (Lv)

Your Level (Lv) is an at-a-glance view of your overall progression and participation in KeyForge organized play through KeyChain, earned over time by accruing Experience Points (XP). Your level appears on your player profile, and additional personalization items can be unlocked through leveling up. Higher levels generally require more XP to level up.

Experience Points (XP)

Experience Points (XP) are earned for general participation in KeyForge organized play across all playgroups, and serve as the foundation for your overall KeyChain account progression. Earning XP is how you level up. XP is awarded as such:

  • Event Attended: 25 XP
  • Match Won: 10 XP
  • Match Lost: 5 XP

Ranking Points (RP)

Ranking Points (RP) are a leaderboard ranking metric used for determining player placements and standings across events. There are global leaderboards, as well as leaderboards within individual playgroups.

Each leaderboard has associated periods, or timeframes, over which players will collect RP and hope to be crowned champion. Currently, there are three different period lengths supported in KeyChain:

  • Monthly leaderboards collect RP earned between the first and last day of a given month.
  • Season leaderboard periods span an entire year, from December 1st through November 30th of the following year. These are timed to coincide with Ghost Galaxyâ€™s organized play schedule, ending just after KeyForge Celebration.
  • All Time leaderboards have a single period with no end date.

In order for RP to be considered for a given leaderboard, it must be earned and recorded between the start and end (plus one week) of the relevant period.
